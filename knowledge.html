Application Form Guide

Confused by the technical terms? Here is a breakdown of the ENEA application fields.

PPE (Punkt Poboru Energii) is your unique electricity meter identification number.

It is required in Section 6 of the ENEA form[cite: 73]. You can find it on your electricity bill, it usually starts with "590...".

In Section 6, you must specify your connection type[cite: 75].

- 1-Phase (Jednofazowa): Typically for older apartments or small loads (under 3-5 kW).
- 3-Phase (Trójfazowa): Standard for modern homes and required for installations above 3.68 kW.

If you selected a 10kW system, you almost certainly have a 3-Phase connection.

This refers to Section 5 of the form "Łączna moc zainstalowana" [cite: 64-65].

- Microinstallation: Any system up to 50 kW.
- Request vs Notification: If your new solar power exceeds your current "Connection Power" (Moc Przyłączeniowa), you need to file a "Request for Conditions" [cite: 5] instead of a simple notification[cite: 4].

This determines your status in Section 10[cite: 103].

- Household: You are a "Consumer-Prosumer". You use energy for living needs. VAT is often lower (8%).
- Business: You use energy for commercial activity. You are a "Business-Prosumer".

Checking "Household" automatically fills the "I am a Prosumer" declaration in Section 7 [cite: 78-79].

Fire Safety & Resources

Essential documents and safety regulations for larger installations.

According to Polish Building Law (Art. 29 ust. 4 pkt 3c) [cite: 219-220], any photovoltaic installation exceeding 6.5 kW in capacity requires:

1. Expert Agreement: The design must be agreed upon with a Certified Fire Safety Expert (Rzeczoznawca ds. zabezpieczeń ppoż)[cite: 242].
2. PSP Notification: A formal notice must be sent to the State Fire Service (Państwowa Straż Pożarna) [cite: 217-218].

Polar Users: If you select a 10 kW system, we automatically flag your application for this expert review process. 5 kW systems are exempt.
